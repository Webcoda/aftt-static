Finding Industry Success in the USA

Finding success within the American Film Industry was no easy feat, but Hale Appleman has shown us how! Join our latest Masterclass host as he blurs the line between what your pipe dream looks like and how you can translate it into reality!

 

Hale Isaac Appleman is an American actor known for his roles as Tobey Cobb in the 2007 film Teeth and Eliot in the television fantasy series The Magicians. Graduate of famed LaGuardia High School of the Performing Arts. Studied at Carnegie Mellon University's School of Drama, he's had his fair share of acting knowledge, tips and tricks bestowed upon him.

Appleman's credits on stage include the revival of Streamers at the Roundabout Theater Company, Clifford Odets' Paradise Lost at the American Repertory Theater, and the New York premiere of Sarah Ruhl's Passion Play as Jesus. In 2011, he played Bob in Moonchildren at the Berkshire Theatre Festival, and can be heard on the L.A. Theatre Works recording of Sam Shepard's Buried Child. Appleman was seen at The Old Globe Theater as Mercutio in The Last Goodbye. Appleman played Zach on the NBC musical drama series Smash.

Hale also made his film debut in Beautiful Ohio and portrayed cartoonist Judd Winick in Pedro. His other credits include Mercutio in Private Romeo, the short film Oysters Rockefeller, and The Magicians, a TV series based on the novel of the same name by Lev Grossman, on which he has played The Magician 'Eliot' since 2015.

Join Hale for an exclusive Masterclass opportunity, diving into the American Film and TV Industry and how you can follow in his footsteps.
